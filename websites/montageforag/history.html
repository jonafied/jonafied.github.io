While new data sets can be easily added, there are forward-thinking companies whose customers can easily share the data they own with other systems.

Montage for Ag pulls in the data generated by these systems:

Trimark
CIMIS

Aerial Ag Imagery’s solution utilizes high-resolution images taken by our manned aircraft, which is equipped with multi spectral imaging (visual and infrared spectrum technology). Our innovative service combines flight collection data with AAI’s proprietary software to provide robust data that can be used to manage pests, nitrogen uptake, water, and other resources more efficiently.

Wiseconn's Dropcontrol offers the most reliable and complete online irrigation management platform in the market. We provide growers real time irrigation system monitoring and control, soil moisture monitoring, and water quality and ground water level monitoring on wells.

"We design and install high-performance solar PV systems that offset the electrical demands of agricultural production while helping customers to reduce operational expenses, improve their bottom line, and farm more sustainably."

Trimark delivers a unified SCADA solution for controlling, monitoring, reporting and analyzing PV generation performance. Trimark’s turnkey solution includes software, hardware, engineering, panel fabrication, installation, and testing. It’s everything you need to move from concept to commissioning.

Customers no longer have to log in to their online account to repeatedly download and send Green Button files. Instead, once a customer authorizes your company to access their PG&E account data, the transfer is handled automatically and repeatedly using standards-based Application Programming Interfaces (APIs). The Share My Data platform packages up the customer’s data, notifies your system when the data is ready and securely sends it.

The California Irrigation Management Information System (CIMIS) is a program unit in the Water Use and Efficiency Branch, Division of Statewide Integrated Water Management, California Department of Water Resources (DWR) that manages a network of over 145 automated weather stations in California. CIMIS was developed in 1982 by DWR and the University of California, Davis (UC Davis). It was designed to assist irrigators in managing their water resources more efficiently. Efficient use of water resources benefits Californians by saving water, energy, and money.
